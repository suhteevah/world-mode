### Key Lessons
- Planning the jump from 7,500 to 10,000 SPM requires calculating exact resource needs for each science type
- A single missing underground belt caused massive uneven loading problems; tiny build errors have outsized impacts at scale
- Red circuits need dedicated allocation tracking because they serve multiple consumers (blue circuits, purple science, space science)
- Green circuits needed +4 more production modules to support 10K SPM (calculated: (5,508 - 4,320) / 360 = +3.3, rounded to +4)
- Accidentally interrupting science production while fixing errors causes visible dips in the production graph

### Design Principles
- Before scaling, calculate exact resource needs per science type and compare against current production capacity
- Track dedicated vs shared resource allocations separately; red circuits allocated to blue science, purple science, and space science must be counted individually
- Use the copy tool to verify build integrity; an odd number of underground belts reveals a missing piece
- Steel needs +3 additional builds for 10K SPM; plastic is already overproduced; stone bricks are sufficient after southern expansion
- Blue circuits and copper are the two critical shortages at the 10K transition point

### Ratios & Numbers
- Green circuits needed for 10K: +4 modules beyond current capacity (5,508 demand vs 4,320 supply, difference / 360 per module)
- Steel: +3 additional builds needed for 10K SPM
- Red circuits: allocated specifically to blue science (2 builds), purple science, and space science
- Green circuit provider stations: 13-14 stations
- Copper demand for 10K: ~20 train loads vs ~11 train loads supplied (massive deficit)
- Plastic: already overproduced, no additional builds needed

### Mistakes to Avoid
- A single missing underground belt can cause all downstream production to load unevenly; always verify builds with the copy tool
- Do not forget to account for dedicated allocations when calculating total resource needs; shared pool numbers are misleading
