## Summary

After adding 2,500 space science, Nilaus faces cascading resource deficits. The demand chain escalates: space science draws blue circuits, which draw red circuits and green circuits, which draw iron, copper, and plastic. Even with ~100 mining productivity, ore deposits are visibly depleting. The episode is a battle to suppress resource warnings by adding a 4th plastic build, more iron, more blue circuits, more green circuits, and more copper. He discovers that copper -- previously assumed to never be a problem -- is now in deficit. The base has ~452 trains and nearly 1,000 stations.

### Key Lessons
- Scaling science creates cascading demand: space science -> blue circuits -> red/green circuits -> raw materials
- Mining productivity does NOT prevent depletion; it only increases output per ore, not ore quantity
- "Never a problem" resources (copper) can suddenly become critical when you scale by 50%
- Buffer fill time creates temporary deficits that look like permanent shortages -- let the base run 1-2 hours before panicking
- Purple science going offline can be caused by upstream buffer starvation, not actual production deficit

### Design Principles
- Build monitoring for every intermediate product, not just raw materials
- When adding a new production tier, expect the tier below it to need expansion too
- Let the base stabilize for 1-2 hours after major additions before diagnosing real vs temporary deficits
- Stockpile warnings (low buffer) are different from urgent warnings (production stopped) -- treat them differently

### Ratios & Numbers
- Science jumped from ~5.4/s to ~8.1/s space science production
- 452 trains in the network, ~1,000 stations
- Copper: 26 trains, still not enough
- Iron: showing deficit warnings despite ~100 mining productivity
- Train travel distance becoming a factor for furthest-away smelting locations
- Purple science discovered offline unexpectedly during the episode

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not assume any resource is "never a problem" -- always plan for scaling demand
- Avoid diagnosing deficits immediately after scaling -- wait for buffers to fill
- Do not forget to add trains when adding new production locations
- Green circuit buffer fill creates massive temporary copper/iron demand that looks like a permanent deficit
