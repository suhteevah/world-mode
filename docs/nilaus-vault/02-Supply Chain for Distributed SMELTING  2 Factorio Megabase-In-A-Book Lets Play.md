## Summary
### Key Lessons
- The transition to megabase is about gradually replacing in-base production with distributed, outsourced production connected via trains
- Moving smelting outside the base frees up space and allows the smelted plates to serve ANY consumer on the train network, not just the original base
- Scaling up is literally copy-paste: stamp down another smelter blueprint, and trains auto-allocate via the many-to-many train network
- Converting old 1-4 trains to 1-8-1 trains is a necessary step; 1-4 trains CAN use 1-8-1 stations at reduced capacity as a temporary bridge

### Design Principles
- Build a mining station template that can be stamped on any ore patch: two loading stations with 4-lane stacker, 8-to-8 balancer feeding trains
- Provider stations auto-calculate train limits based on chest contents (16,000 ore per train, max 3 trains per station)
- Requester stations monitor chest contents and request trains when buffer drops below threshold (e.g., 144,000 for copper plates = ~4.5 trainloads)
- Use 8-to-8 balancers even when you only need 6 lanes out - better balancing than using a 6-lane balancer
- Reuse blueprints obsessively: the unloader pattern, the provider/requester circuit logic, the stacker design

### Ratios & Numbers
- Copper plate requester: 32 boxes x 4,800 capacity = 153,600 max; set request to 144,000 (leaves room for one full train to always unload)
- Copper plate train: 32,000 per train (8 wagons x 4,000 at stack 100)
- Ore train: 16,000 per train (8 wagons x 2,000 at stack 50)
- Mining station: 2 loading bays with 4-lane stacker, 3 trains per station
- Base input station: 6 lanes feeding into the bus via 8-to-8 balancer (using only 6 of 8 outputs)
- One smelter block produces 8 belts of copper plates; the base consumes more, so multiple smelter blocks needed

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not mix old train sizes (1-4) with new (1-8-1) on the same schedule unless you understand the capacity reduction
- Do not forget to set correct train limit parameters when copying provider/requester blueprints to new stations
- Always place a chain signal before the stacker entrance for better train routing decisions
- Do not delay replacing old in-base smelting - the sooner you outsource, the sooner you free capacity for scaling
