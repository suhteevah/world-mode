- Blue science is built at 2,700/min (half scale) first, then doubled later - red and green were small enough to build at full 5,400/min immediately
- Blue science needs 4 inbound resources: iron, steel, red circuits, sulfur - plus 1 outbound
- Standardized unload station blueprint ("unload 4 lanes") created for reuse across future builds
- Future sciences (yellow, purple, space) will need multiple city blocks each due to complexity
- Spidertron armies no longer need artillery trains - they are more effective for clearing biters

### Design Principles
- Build at half-scale (2,700/min) first for complex sciences, then duplicate to reach 5,400/min
- Standardize station blueprints: create "generic unload station" that works for any 4-inbound build
- Blue science location: near red circuit production to minimize transport distance for its biggest input
- Every build that is different gets designed fresh; builds that are the same get copy-pasted
- Always keep robots busy - queue up the next build phase while current one is still being constructed

### Ratios & Numbers
- Blue science target: 2,700 per minute (45 per second) at half scale
- Blue science inputs: iron, steel, red circuits, sulfur (4 inbound + 1 outbound)
- Standard unload station: 4 lane unload pattern, reusable blueprint
- Sulfur trains: 2 trains created for first-time sulfur delivery to blue science
- Blue circuit buffer: 2,000+ at science destination before trains can deliver

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Forgetting to create dedicated trains for new resources (sulfur had zero trains initially)
- Two trains routing to the same station when a second station was available - caused by station not being active when train was dispatched
- Using blue inserters in assembler builds when standard inserters are sufficient
- Not having the standardized unload blueprint earlier - would have saved repeated manual setup
