## Summary

Nilaus designs a direct train-to-train steel smelting facility, following the same philosophy as the plastic builds. He adds two more copper smelting facilities (68 copper stations, 69 trains) to address copper deficits. The new steel design uses iron ore trains feeding directly into electric furnaces that output steel into outbound steel trains. Each new steel facility produces 104 steel/second using ~8 belts of iron, compared to 66/s from old belt-based builds (1.5x improvement). He also begins artillery expansion to push back biters and access new iron deposits.

### Key Lessons
- Train-to-train steel produces 1.5x more per facility than belt-based steel (104/s vs 66/s)
- The real advantage is UPS: eliminating half-entry belts, splitters, and non-full belt segments
- Blue circuits are the slowest to build stockpile -- they take many episodes to stabilize even with 6+ facilities
- Artillery range upgrades are needed to access new ore deposits for continued scaling
- Copper went from "never a problem" to needing 68+ stations

### Design Principles
- Train-to-train steel pivots around the steel output train as the central element
- Use beacons reaching across to both furnace rows to maximize module coverage
- One beacon hitting 3+ machines is the threshold for placing it
- Lights cost UPS but are worth it for visibility during construction
- When constrained on space, accept non-standard layouts (e.g., 2 trains on one side, 1 on the other)

### Ratios & Numbers
- New steel facility: 104 steel/second, using ~8 belts of iron ore
- Old steel facility: 66 steel/second each
- New facility = 1.5x old facility output
- 68 copper stations, 69 copper trains
- 7 steel output stations per new facility
- 52 modules needed per steel facility
- Blue circuits still not fully buffered despite 6 production facilities
- Iron and copper deficits stabilizing after adding new smelting

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not neglect beacon placement optimization -- moving beacons one tile can affect coverage of 3+ machines
- Avoid building in constrained spaces without first confirming all trains fit
- Blue circuit warnings may persist for many real-time hours while buffers fill -- this is normal, not a crisis
- Do not forget to verify "no ingredients" status on machines to confirm input starvation vs output backup
