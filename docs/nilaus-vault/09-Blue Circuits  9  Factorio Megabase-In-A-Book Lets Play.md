## Summary
### Key Lessons
- Blue circuits (processing units) are the final circuit tier needed before module production can be outsourced
- Blue circuits activate many idle builds: the green circuit outpost finally gets real demand, sulfuric acid from episode 8 gets consumed
- The oil refining blueprint from episode 8 had errors: one pump produced over 1,200 fluid (exceeding single-pump capacity), requiring an additional pump line
- Keep the in-base oil refinery running alongside the outsourced one - it feeds the home base while the new one feeds future builds
- Lubricant production from the oil block should also supply the home base (belts consume lubricant)

### Design Principles
- Blue circuit block: 3 city blocks (stackers, stations, production) - same pattern as other builds
- Inputs: green circuits, red circuits, sulfuric acid via train
- Output: blue circuits (processing units) via train
- Deliberately offset station positions from intersection alignment to avoid four-way intersections (which cause congestion)
- Sulfuric acid is the critical enabler: needed for blue circuits AND batteries (accumulators for solar power)
- Build toward solar power transition: nuclear is temporary, solar + accumulators is the endgame power solution

### Ratios & Numbers
- Blue circuit recipe: 20 green circuits + 2 red circuits + 5 sulfuric acid per craft, 10 second craft time
- Oil refinery fix: each pump handles max ~1,200 fluid/sec; the build needed a second pump line to handle demand
- Speed modules remaining: only ~300 in stock, rapidly depleting - module factory is urgent
- Build order so far: copper -> iron -> steel -> green circuits -> plastic -> red circuits -> oil/sulfur/lube -> blue circuits

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not remove the in-base oil refinery when building the outsourced one; keep both running for different consumers
- Do not align train stations directly with grid intersections - offset them to avoid creating four-way junctions
- Do not underestimate sulfuric acid consumption; blue circuits use significant amounts
- Blueprint errors compound: always verify pump throughput against actual demand before stamping down multiples
