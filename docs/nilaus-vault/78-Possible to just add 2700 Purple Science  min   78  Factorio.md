## Summary

Nilaus works on adding ~2,700 purple science/min to reach 7,500 SPM. He discovers that purple science mostly needs red circuits and steel, with minimal green circuit demand (only for productivity modules). He retires dedicated green circuit builds from the purple science area since they can draw from the global network instead. A key UPS insight: the debug setting "show transport line gap" reveals that non-full belts create vastly more entities than full belts. The old steel build's half-empty belts are a major UPS concern. He also fixes a satellite delivery bug where trains got stuck because unload conditions were too demanding.

### Key Lessons
- Purple science primarily needs red circuits and steel; green circuit demand is minimal
- Non-full belts are the biggest UPS killer: each gap in items creates a separate transport line entity
- Dedicated sub-component builds (green circuits for purple science) are wasteful when global supply exists
- The "show transport line gap" debug setting visually demonstrates why partial belts destroy UPS
- Steel will become the next major bottleneck for 7,500 SPM

### Design Principles
- Draw common intermediates (green circuits) from global supply rather than dedicated local builds
- For purple science, build only: purple science assemblers + red circuits + steel supply
- Blue circuit stockpile takes extremely long to build -- do not add more consumers until buffers stabilize
- Fix train delivery conditions to avoid deadlocks: set wagon content threshold below maximum to ensure trains can always unload

### Ratios & Numbers
- Purple science for 2,500 SPM: needs 123 red circuits (full module), minimal green circuits
- Iron supply: only providing 4 stations when 27 copper stations were requested (16 copper locations not being picked up)
- Steel consumption: old builds using only ~5 belts of iron, not the expected 8
- Blue circuits: 6 production facilities, still not building stockpile
- Still consuming raw materials equivalent to 7,500 SPM just filling buffers
- Satellite delivery fix: threshold changed to 48 (from 60) to prevent unload deadlock

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not build dedicated green circuit production for purple science -- wasteful duplication
- Avoid adding yellow science before blue circuits stabilize -- yellow consumes blue
- Non-full belt segments are computationally expensive; prefer full or empty belts
- Train unload conditions must account for the case where destination storage cannot accept a full load
