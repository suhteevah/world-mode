## Summary

Nilaus completes scaling steel production to match 7,500 SPM requirements. He builds 4 new train-to-train steel facilities and calculates total steel production: 13 old facilities x 66/s = 864/s + 4 new facilities x 104/s = 416/s, totaling 1,280 steel/second. ### Key Lessons
- Steel for 7,500 SPM: need 1,220/second; achieved 1,280/second (only 60/s margin, ~5%)
- Blue circuits are the last bottleneck to clear -- once they stabilize, the base is ready for final science scaling
- Iron/copper have stabilized after adding new smelting; plastic nearly stable; blue circuits are the holdout
- Artillery range upgrades are critical for accessing new ore deposits to sustain megabase growth
- Solar panel construction competes with science production for steel

### Design Principles
- Calculate total production by summing old + new facility outputs, then compare to SPM requirements
- A 5% margin on critical resources is tight but workable if you have stockpile buffers
- Idle blue circuit trains are the signal that production has finally caught up with demand
- 58 steel stations means 58 steel trains needed for full saturation (1 train per station)
- Decommission old builds only after confirming new builds meet total demand

### Ratios & Numbers
- Total steel: 1,280/second (13 old x 66/s + 4 new x 104/s)
- 7,500 SPM steel requirement: 1,220/second
- Steel surplus: 60/second (~5% margin)
- 58 total steel stations requiring 58 steel trains
- 32 new steel stations (4 facilities x 8 stations each)
- 26 old steel stations (13 facilities x 2 stations each)
- Blue circuits: approaching full buffer at 32,000 per location
- Remaining to upgrade for 7,500 SPM: yellow science and blue science

### Mistakes to Avoid
- A 5% margin on steel is dangerously thin if solar panel construction continues consuming steel
- Do not start yellow science until blue circuits are fully stable -- yellow consumes blue
- Verify all new steel facilities are "fully operational" before counting their output in calculations
- 58 trains needed just for steel -- plan train network capacity accordingly
