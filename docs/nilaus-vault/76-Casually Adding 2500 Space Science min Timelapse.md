## Summary

Nilaus scales from 5,000 to 7,500 SPM by "casually" adding 2,500 space science/min. He performs detailed calculations for what each additional 2,500 SPM requires: additional rocket fuel modules, rocket control units, low density structures, and red/green circuit builds. The episode demonstrates how having templates ready makes scaling straightforward -- just calculate how many additional modules of each component, place them, and connect. He also decommissions old plastic infrastructure by setting train limits to zero and draining remaining inventory.

### Key Lessons
- When templates are ready, adding 2,500 SPM is mostly a calculation and placement exercise
- Always round up when calculating required production modules -- 5.1 becomes 6
- Build sub-components (low density structures, rocket fuel) before the final assembly (space science) to avoid exhausting upstream supplies
- Decommission old builds by setting train limits to zero and letting existing inventory drain naturally

### Design Principles
- Calculate requirements in items/second, then map to module counts (always round up to whole modules)
- Build low density structures first when adding space science -- this draws in excess plastic
- Science is measured in SPM (per minute) but all other rates use items/second -- convert carefully
- Use existing production module benchmarks: measure one module's actual output and extrapolate

### Ratios & Numbers
- Target: 2,500 additional space science/min (total to ~7,500 SPM)
- Rocket fuel: 64 needed, producing 65/s (3,900/min / 60) -- need +2 modules
- Rocket control units: 32 needed, need +1 module
- Each rocket fuel module produces ~17.4/s
- Low density structures: need 30 for 2,500 SPM
- Red/green circuits: dedicated builds needed for each space science cluster
- 6 old plastic stations decommissioned (set train limit to 0)
- 7 plastic trains for new system, 6 for draining old system

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not start space science assembly before sub-components (rocket fuel, LDS) are producing -- you will exhaust upstream supplies
- Mixing SPM (per minute) with items/second calculations causes errors
- Do not forget to actually drain old production facilities before decommissioning trains
- Water supply for rocket fuel can become a hidden bottleneck at scale
