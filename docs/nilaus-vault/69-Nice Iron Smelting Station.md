## Summary

### Key Lessons
- Transitioning iron smelting: upper ore patches convert to new direct-mining modules, lower patches convert to steel production
- Enable/disable vs train limits: enable/disable skips the station when closed; train limits make trains WAIT indefinitely for the station to open -- a critical distinction that caused a satellite production deadlock
- Converting green circuit builds to use the new iron supply first validates the new system with a real consumer
- Beacons and speed modules are the constant bottleneck when deploying new smelting modules at scale

### Design Principles
- Plan resource transitions geographically: upper patches = iron (new modules), lower patches = steel (old style converted)
- Validate new production systems by connecting them to real consumers (green circuits) before decommissioning old systems
- Use enable/disable for stations in multi-stop train schedules where you want trains to skip when not needed
- Use train limits for stations where you want trains to queue and wait for availability
- Build trains at the deployment site rather than shipping them across the map -- saves time and avoids main line congestion

### Ratios & Numbers
- Each new iron module: 723 plates/second (twice the output of old smelting stations)
- Satellite train stuck for multiple episodes due to enable/disable vs train limit confusion
- 6 city blocks minimum needed for a large ore-to-smelting conversion -- cannot fit in constrained spaces
- Science dropped from 5K to nearly zero during the satellite production deadlock
- Need 9+ trains per iron module, plus beacons, speed modules, and fuel infrastructure

### Mistakes to Avoid
- CRITICAL: Do not use "set train limit" when you mean "enable/disable" -- trains will wait indefinitely at a closed station, blocking the entire schedule
- Do not decommission old iron smelting before new modules AND their consumers are fully operational
- Beacon and module shortages will stall new construction -- plan production of these components ahead of deployment
- Monitor satellite production closely during any infrastructure transition; it is the most fragile link in the science chain
