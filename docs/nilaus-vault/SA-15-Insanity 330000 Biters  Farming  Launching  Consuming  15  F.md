## Summary
### Key Lessons
- 330,000 biter eggs must be farmed, launched to orbit, and consumed for Promethium science
- Biter eggs (berex) have 30-minute expiry; entire production chain must sync to this timer
- Stability is the most important word for Promethium science production
- Even at 1M SPM, each research productivity level takes ~7 hours (14 hours real time at reduced UPS)

### Space Age Mechanics
- Biter egg farming on Gleba: capture, incubate, manage safely
- Biter eggs spoil after 30 minutes; must be consumed before expiry
- Launch biter eggs to orbit and consume on Promethium ships
- If eggs hatch, they destroy infrastructure; safety systems are mandatory

### Design Principles
- Balance farming rate with launch rate with consumption rate
- Stable rhythm is more important than peak throughput
- Ship cycling must maintain consistent cadence
- 10-hour stable production verified at 1M SPM

### Ratios & Numbers
- 330,000 biter eggs per production cycle
- 1,000,000 science per minute sustained for 10+ hours
- ~7 hours per research productivity level at 1M SPM
- 14 hours real time due to UPS reduction

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Biter eggs hatching is catastrophic; never let buffer overflow
- Do not break the 30-minute cycle timing
- Stability over throughput; a crashed system produces zero science
- Account for UPS impact when calculating real-time research durations
