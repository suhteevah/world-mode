## Summary
### Key Lessons
- Transition from a normal base (300 SPM) to megabase (target 2,700 SPM) by outsourcing production into modular city-block designs
- Start with copper smelting first (simplest to replace), then iron, then steel, then circuits
- Use a "Base in a Book" approach: every design fits into city-block-sized blueprints that can be stamped down anywhere on a train network
- The series is NOT about UPS-optimized designs; it focuses on modular, repeatable, train-based builds

### Design Principles
- City blocks provide standardized sizing so blueprints are interchangeable (1x2, 1x3, 2x6 blocks etc.)
- Use 1-8-1 trains (one locomotive, eight wagons, one locomotive) instead of 2-8 to fit within city block constraints and allow bidirectional travel
- 8-to-8 balancers with throughput-unlimited and lane-balancing properties are preferred
- Every build location must handle its own fueling from day one (dedicated fuel train stop with roboport)
- Keep roboport networks isolated per city block to prevent accidental merging
- Use chain signals before stackers so trains recalculate routing at the last moment
- At megabase scale, just use stack inserters (green) everywhere - don't bother carrying blue inserters
- Design process: determine build size first, then figure out I/O, then train scheduling, then fill in details

### Ratios & Numbers
- Train configuration: 1-8-1 (locomotive-wagon-locomotive)
- Smelting design: consumes less than 1 belt inbound, produces 8 belts outbound
- Ore train capacity: 16,000 per train (8 wagons x 2,000 ore per wagon at stack size 50)
- Plate train capacity: 32,000 per train (8 wagons x 4,000 plates per wagon at stack size 100)
- Requester station buffer: 72,000 (set below max chest capacity of 76,800 to ensure trains can always fully unload)
- 32 boxes per unloading station, each holding 2,400 = 76,800 max capacity
- Rule of thumb: 3 trains per mining pickup location
- Stacker depth: 3 trains per station side

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not place signals at the edges of city blocks - trains can block intersections; place signals mid-block instead
- Do not starve upstream supply chains - always ensure mining > smelting > consumption capacity
- Four-way intersections are more susceptible to congestion than T-intersections
- Do not build smelting arrays before verifying the train scheduling and space constraints fit
