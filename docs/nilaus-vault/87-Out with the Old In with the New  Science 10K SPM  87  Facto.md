## Summary

### Key Lessons
- At extreme scale, the minimap becomes the primary gameplay view; zoomed-in views provide no useful information
- Copper shortage becomes a real crisis at 10K scale: providing ~11 train loads but demanding ~20 (nearly 2x deficit)
- Steel needs to be updated and scaled before the 10K transition; running out of steel cascades into multiple science types
- Iron and copper warnings that previously seemed "fake" become genuine shortages at higher demand levels
- The nature of episodes changes at this scale; time is spent more on debugging and planning than on building

### Design Principles
- Monitor the train system from the zoomed-out map view; train jams are visible as clusters of stopped trains
- Use the calculation summary from previous episodes to track what needs scaling before each upgrade
- When copper or iron demand doubles, consider adding 4+ new smelting sites rather than trying to optimize existing ones
- Old builds that were designed for lower throughput should be replaced ("out with the old, in with the new") rather than patched

### Ratios & Numbers
- Copper supply: ~11 train loads provided
- Copper demand: ~20 train loads demanded (nearly 2x shortfall)
- Steel: needs full upgrade for 10K SPM support
- Blue science being scaled up in this episode as part of 10K push
- Base runs at approximately 40+ UPS while recording

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not hope copper shortages "solve themselves"; at 2x deficit, active intervention is required
- Do not keep old, inefficient builds running when they could be replaced with properly scaled versions
- Do not underestimate the copper demand of blue circuits + green circuits combined at 10K scale
- Do not plan episodes too rigidly at this scale; debugging takes unpredictable amounts of time
