## Summary
### Key Lessons
- The module factory is the last production build before transitioning to expansion (solar power, landfill, territory clearing)
- With green, red, and blue circuits all outsourced, a dedicated module factory can run continuously using those inputs
- Speed modules are far more consumed than productivity modules - production should be weighted toward speed
- Power is dangerously close to capacity; solar panel expansion is the priority after the module factory
- Train congestion near old mining stations finally caused a deadlock - proper signal spacing is critical even for "temporary" builds

### Design Principles
- Module factory block: 3 inputs (green, red, blue circuits), 1 output (modules) - fits the standard 3-in/1-out station template
- Produces both speed modules 3 and productivity modules 3 simultaneously, weighted toward speed
- Once module factory is running, deconstruct the in-base module production to free blue circuits for other uses
- Solar expansion requires: solar panels, accumulators, landfill, and cleared territory (biters)
- Expansion plan: clear territory in one direction first, then fill with solar arrays

### Ratios & Numbers
- Module factory inputs: green circuits (high volume), red circuits (medium), blue circuits (lower volume)
- Speed module 3 recipe: 5 red circuits + 5 blue circuits + 1 speed module 2
- Productivity module 3 recipe: 5 red circuits + 5 blue circuits + 1 productivity module 2
- Landfill stockpile: only 5-6k per chest, insufficient for large solar expansion
- Power: nearly at max nuclear capacity, no headroom for new production builds without solar

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not cram train stations too close together without proper signal spacing - it WILL cause deadlocks (demonstrated live in this episode)
- Fix for deadlocks: add chain signals so trains cannot enter a block until they can exit it
- Do not delay module production - every new build requires speed/productivity modules, and running out halts progress
- Do not forget that solar expansion needs not just panels but also accumulators (batteries -> sulfuric acid -> oil processing chain)
- The real fix for congested old mining areas is to decommission them entirely rather than bandaid with signals
