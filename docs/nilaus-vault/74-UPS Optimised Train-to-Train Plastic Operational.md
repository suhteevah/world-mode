## Summary

Nilaus implements and operationalizes his train-to-train plastic design. He builds the production facility next to oil deposits (rather than coal) since oil has no other uses. The episode covers the full construction process: placing train stations, connecting oil pipelines with limited buffer tanks, solving fluid balancing issues, and powering the facility. He discovers that fluid throughput with 6 pipe segments between pumps yields only ~1500/s per line, which is insufficient. The preliminary UPS comparison shows a factor of 2-5x reduction in active entities compared to the belt-based plastic builds.

### Key Lessons
- Build plastic facilities next to oil deposits (not coal), since coal is used for other things but oil is dedicated to plastic
- Trains as fluid distribution medium solve the notoriously difficult problem of balancing fluids across many consumers
- Idle trains are computationally cheap; pathfinding over short distances is also cheap
- Non-full belts are UPS-expensive because each gap creates a separate entity for the game to calculate
- Oil pipe throughput is limited: ~1500/s with 6 pipe segments between pumps

### Design Principles
- Use an uneven train station pattern on either side of the build to ensure trains align at the same physical location for proper loading
- Oil buffer should be minimal (one tank, 25,000) to avoid fluid balancing nightmares
- Trains themselves serve as the buffer -- no need for excessive tank storage
- Maximum 6 pipe segments between pumps for adequate throughput
- Chain signals at train station entrances must be on the same side for both directions

### Ratios & Numbers
- Base stable at 5,000 SPM before this build
- 1 million solar panels reached, targeting 1 million accumulators next
- Oil facilities: 3,700 to 4,500 per station, with some up to 66,000
- Pipe throughput: ~1,500/s per line with 6 segments between pumps
- UPS improvement: preliminary factor of 5x fewer active entities (2-3x when counting pipes)
- Old plastic build: 1,900 belt entities (most non-full = active)

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not start trains unbalanced -- fluid imbalance without balancers perpetuates forever
- Pipe segment count matters enormously for throughput; exceeding 6 segments kills flow rate
- Do not forget to disable inbound signals while building to prevent premature train routing
- Forgetting to kill radar/obstacles before placing blueprints wastes time
