## Summary

### Key Lessons
- 10,000 SPM achieved: the series goal from episode 1 is complete at episode 91
- Three factors enable 10K SPM at 40+ UPS: (1) Factorio's engine optimization by Wube, (2) UPS-conscious base design using trains/direct insertion/full belts, (3) good hardware
- Before the final scaling push, all warnings must be eliminated and all buffers fully saturated
- Copper was the final resource requiring expansion (3 new smelting sites: copper #9, #10, #11)
- Additional plastic facility was needed even though calculations said it should be sufficient; real-world throughput differs from theoretical

### Design Principles
- The three pillars of megabase UPS performance: engine optimization, UPS-conscious design, capable hardware
- UPS-conscious design means: heavy train reliance, direct insertion, full belts, minimal robots, minimal splitters
- Ensure zero dashboard warnings before attempting the final science scaling; any active warning will cascade
- Build surplus capacity for plastic and copper before scaling; these are the resources most likely to cause intermittent failures
- Train congestion is the next potential failure mode after achieving 10K SPM

### Ratios & Numbers
- Final achievement: 10,000 science per minute across all 7 science types
- UPS while recording: 40-41 updates per second (higher without recording)
- Copper expansion: 3 new smelting sites (#9, #10, #11) added to eliminate copper deficit
- Plastic: +13 idle trains in network after adding another production facility
- Total series length: 91 episodes from first rail to 10K SPM
- Base designed entirely around train logistics with city-block tile system

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not forget to hook up newly moved stations; a disconnected station silently fails to produce
- Do not assume theoretical calculations perfectly predict real throughput; always verify with actual production data
- Do not attempt to double from 10K to 20K; the UPS cost and network congestion make it impractical
- Train congestion will be the next bottleneck to monitor; it did not break the base at 10K but could at higher scales
