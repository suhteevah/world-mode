## Summary

### Key Lessons
- Iron, blue circuits, and plastic are the recurring bottleneck triad at 7,500+ SPM
- Green circuit shortages are often a downstream symptom of iron shortages, not a green circuit capacity problem
- Massive southern expansion is necessary to access new iron and oil patches for scaling beyond 7,500 SPM
- New production takes ~10 minutes to fully saturate train buffers and start delivering consistently
- Train network crowding becomes visible at this scale; the network itself starts constraining throughput

### Design Principles
- Expand resource production geographically rather than trying to squeeze more from existing patches
- Train allocation rule: one train per inbound station plus extras for long travel distances
- For high-throughput resources (iron), some stations run 17 trains to 4 stations for maximum throughput
- Build new smelting/production facilities near resource patches (on-site smelting) to reduce train congestion

### Ratios & Numbers
- Iron expansion: +2,800 iron/s from new southern facilities
- Plastic expansion: +2,000 plastic/s from new southern oil processing
- Total trains: 843 allocated to 1,231 stations
- Iron trains: 100 trains to 90 stations (roughly 1:1 plus extras)
- Plastic trains: 50 trains to 45 stations
- Green circuit builds: 12 in one area, 17 in another (29 total)
- UPS: 45 frames per second (with recording overhead)
- New smelting fills buffers in approximately 10 minutes before trains start departing full

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not assume green circuit shortages are a green circuit problem; trace upstream to iron supply first
- Do not undercount train allocation for distant stations; long travel time means trains spend more time in transit
- Stone brick shortages can appear suddenly when new builds come online; keep surplus capacity
- The base getting "crowded" with trains is a warning sign of network saturation approaching
