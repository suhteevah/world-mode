## Summary
### Key Lessons
- Train network congestion is the #1 killer for megabase scaling - everything must be done to minimize train count
- On-site smelting eliminates both ore trains AND plate trains, replacing them with direct ore-to-plate at the mine
- Moving smelting on-site reduced copper trains from 24 (9 ore + 15 plate) to far fewer direct plate trains
- Column-based layout: group all builds for one science type in a vertical column to minimize cross-base train traffic
- Transitioning from centralized to on-site smelting is painful (temporary copper shortage) but necessary for scale

### Design Principles
- On-site smelting: place smelters directly at ore patches, output plates onto trains instead of raw ore
- Column layout for sciences: all intermediates for blue science (green circuits, plastic, red circuits, sulfur, steel) in one column, feeding directly into blue science at the bottom
- When on-site smelters can produce 8 blue belts out but only consume 6.7, the surplus handles any throughput variations
- With productivity modules and 47 million ore, on-site patches effectively never run out
- Iron-to-steel conversion should also be on-site wherever steel is consumed (blue science, purple science)

### Ratios & Numbers
- Pre-transition: 9 copper ore trains + 15 copper plate trains = 24 trains for copper alone
- Post-transition target: far fewer trains carrying plates directly from on-site smelters
- Total trains at episode start: 80 trains in network
- Copper smelter output: 8 blue belts, consumption: 6.7 blue belts (surplus of 1.3 belts)
- Dashboard showed demand exceeding supply by 19 train-loads for copper - critical shortage signal
- Ore patch: 47 million copper with productivity bonuses = effectively infinite

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Cutting over all copper production at once causes a temporary base-wide copper shortage - transition incrementally if possible
- Leaving spidertrons stationary in a defensive position drains their ammo without clearing threats
- Not planning column layout before building individual science blocks leads to excessive cross-base train traffic
- Dashboard copper ore alert became irrelevant after on-site smelting transition - update alerts when architecture changes
