## Summary
### Key Lessons
- Red and green science pair well together on one ship: red is copper-heavy, green is iron-heavy
- Cast iron gears recipe does NOT preserve quality; must use old-school gear crafting for legendary
- Molten metal recipes lose quality; use traditional smelting for legendary items
- Balance iron and copper consumption by combining both sciences on one ship

### Space Age Mechanics
- Legendary chunks from space casino become legendary iron/copper plates via traditional smelting
- Quality is preserved through assembler crafting but NOT through foundry/cast recipes
- Each ship produces ~120/sec of both red and green legendary science
- 3-4 ships needed to meet megabase demand

### Design Principles
- Prioritize copper inserter placement to prevent belt jamming from iron overflow
- Use two inserters for critical resource feeds to maintain saturation
- Buffer on belt before science assemblers to prevent ingredient shortage stuttering

### Ratios & Numbers
- 120 legendary red science/sec per ship; 120 legendary green science/sec per ship
- 7,200 per 10 minutes verified production rate
- 60 gears + 60 copper plates + 60 inserters + 60 transport belts per ship
- Iron-to-copper ratio: ~2:1 from metallic chunks

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Never use cast iron gears for legendary production (loses quality)
- Ensure copper output belt is never fully blocked or whole production chain jams
- Do not let iron overflow dominate the shared belt; filter aggressively
