## Summary

### Key Lessons
- On-site smelting (smelting at the mining location rather than a centralized smelter) significantly reduces train traffic -- removing ~20% of trains from the network (from 86 down to 57)
- Converting existing infrastructure is iterative: one step forward, sometimes one step back, then two steps forward
- Steel smelting can reuse the same on-site iron plate smelting blueprint -- just rename stations and let furnaces adapt to iron plate input instead of iron ore
- Use temporary trains to move residual resources (e.g., 63,000 iron plates) between stations during conversion rather than manually picking items up
- Complete infrastructure migrations before starting new science builds to avoid compounding complexity

### Design Principles
- On-site smelting city blocks: fit smelting array + two train stations (loading) inside one city block
- Use ghost placement when prototyping layouts to avoid costly mistakes during iteration
- Trains can only enter from one side in the on-site smelting design -- cannot mirror, only rotate
- Balancing is needed both inside and outside the smelter when there are no splitters to distribute evenly
- Stackers are essential even in compact on-site smelting designs -- don't forget them during layout
- Each science column should have its own dedicated smelting rather than sharing a global smelter

### Ratios & Numbers
- On-site iron/copper smelting: consumes 8 belts of ore inbound
- On-site steel smelting: consumes ~8 belts of iron plates, produces ~1.8 belts of steel (with 20% productivity)
- Steel smelter throughput: 8 belts iron in -> ~87% of 2 belts steel out (close to 2 full belts)
- Productivity bonus on steel: 1.6x multiplier on input (8 belts * 1.2 = 9.6 effective, / 5 for steel ratio = ~1.92 belts)
- 15 furnaces per line for steel (even number arrangement), first and last furnace both at speed 7.4
- Train count reduced from 86 to 57 after full on-site conversion (6 copper + 6 iron stations converted)

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't forget to include stackers when designing compact train station layouts
- Removing chests with residual items is the worst part of cleanup -- plan migrations to let buffers drain first
- Don't start new science column builds until infrastructure migration is complete
- Robot charging bottleneck is real in late game -- roboport charging rate limits construction speed significantly
- When copy-pasting blueprints, verify you didn't miss outer elements (easy to clip edges)
