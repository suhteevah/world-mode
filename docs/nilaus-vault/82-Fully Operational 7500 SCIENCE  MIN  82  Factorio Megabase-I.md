## Summary

### Key Lessons
- Achieving 7,500 SPM requires all seven sciences running simultaneously; yellow science was the final piece
- Blue circuits become the critical bottleneck when yellow science comes online because yellow also consumes blue circuits
- Plastic shortages are often a saturation timing issue, not a true capacity shortfall; patience lets buffers fill
- Adding science labs in pairs makes scaling predictable: each pair adds ~1,250 SPM

### Design Principles
- Scale science facilities incrementally: bring each new lab pair online one at a time and verify supply before adding the next
- Train loading speed matters less than belt throughput; as long as loading is faster than belt consumption, it works
- Module-intensive builds (beacons + speed/productivity modules) are the primary construction bottleneck at megabase scale
- Always verify resource sustainability after scaling: iron, plastic, power, and blue circuits are the usual failure points

### Ratios & Numbers
- Red science: 10,800/min (capacity for 10K+ SPM)
- Green science: 10,800/min
- Blue science: 8,100/min
- Purple science: 8,100/min
- Space science: 8,100/min (three rocket silos, theoretical max ~9,000/min)
- Yellow science: scaled from 5,100 to 7,500/min by adding two more lab pairs
- Each science lab pair contributes approximately 1,250 SPM
- Maximum 12 science delivery trains serving 6 different sciences (2 trains each) at the science facility
- UPS at 44 with base fully operational

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not scale yellow science without first confirming blue circuit supply is sufficient
- Do not forget to bring enough modules and beacons when building remotely; running out mid-build wastes time
- Verify trains go to the correct station after building new labs; trains can route to unintended stops
- Do not skip checking power capacity when all facilities come online simultaneously
