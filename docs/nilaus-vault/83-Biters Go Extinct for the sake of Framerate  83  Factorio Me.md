## Summary

### Key Lessons
- Biters become irrelevant in late-game megabases; they consume UPS without posing any real threat
- Removing biters, disabling pollution, and removing perimeter defenses recovers significant UPS (from ~37 to ~48 UPS)
- Three things to disable for UPS recovery: kill all biters, prevent biter spawning, disable pollution calculation
- Removing artillery outposts and defense infrastructure further frees up UPS since they serve no purpose without biters
- Never remove radar coverage even for UPS savings; flying blind on a megabase is unacceptable

### Design Principles
- UPS optimization is a legitimate design priority at megabase scale; remove anything that does not contribute to science production
- Benchmark UPS by standing in one spot fully zoomed in and letting the game stabilize before measuring
- After removing biters, reclaim territory for resource expansion (iron, oil patches)
- New chunks loaded by exploration cost UPS; avoid unnecessary map exploration

### Ratios & Numbers
- UPS before biter removal: ~37-38 (while recording)
- UPS after biter removal + defense cleanup: ~46-48
- UPS gain from removing biters/pollution/defenses: approximately +10 UPS
- Iron deficit before expansion: -36 train loads
- Southern expansion revealed: 10 iron patches, 10 oil patches available
- Stone trains: needed 6 trains for 4 stations (increased from 4 trains)
- Sulfuric acid: added new production facility to fix blue circuit shortage

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not keep biters active past the point where they are a meaningful threat; the UPS cost is too high
- Do not remove radar coverage for UPS savings; you need visibility to debug train networks
- Do not ignore stone brick shortages; they cascade into purple science failures
- Check train count vs station count ratios; having fewer trains than stations causes intermittent supply failures
