## Summary
### Key Lessons
- Green circuits are the next outsourced production after smelting; they are the highest-volume intermediate product
- The design philosophy is "crisp and repeatable" - boring square designs are intentional because they tile and scale
- Train signals were moved from city block edges to mid-block to prevent intersection blocking (fix from earlier mistake)
- Supply chain integrity is critical: mining must never bottleneck smelting, smelting must never bottleneck circuit production
- Pave over local resources (oil, copper, ore) that conflict with your city block grid - the grid structure is more valuable

### Design Principles
- Green circuit block: takes copper and iron plates inbound via train, outputs 8 belts of green circuits
- Each green circuit tile uses beacon-boosted assembly machines from the master class blueprints

- Once one green circuit block is built, you can copy it adjacent and double production; trains auto-allocate

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not place signals at city block edges - trains will block intersections; mid-block signals are the fix
- Do not underestimate train counts; having too few trains is a silent throughput killer
- Do not ignore the supply chain: if your smelter is starved, your circuit factory starves too, cascading upstream
- Forgetting to connect rail segments or leaving gaps in the grid causes trains to path incorrectly
