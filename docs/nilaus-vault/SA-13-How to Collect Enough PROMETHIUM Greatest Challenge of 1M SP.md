## Summary
### Key Lessons
- Promethium science is the hardest part of the megabase; others skip it but that is cheating
- Promethium requires three things: Promethium asteroid chunks, quantum processors, and biter eggs (berex)
- Hundreds of thousands of biter eggs must be managed safely; logistical orchestration is critical
- Modular ship design: production, power storage, front collection/defense, side collection, storage, fuel, controls
- 4-5 ships needed running in constant cycle

### Space Age Mechanics
- Promethium asteroids only found in deep space beyond normal planetary orbits
- Ships must defend against asteroids while collecting Promethium chunks
- Biter eggs have 30-minute expiry; everything must work on 30-minute cycles
- Particle removal mod helps with UPS performance

### Design Principles
- Modular ship components: each can be customized independently
- Defense modules handle biter eggs that try to hatch on ship
- Ship width and speed determine collection rate
- Fail-safes and rhythm/cadence essential for stable production

### Ratios & Numbers
- 4-5 Promethium collection ships needed for continuous supply
- ~990 Promethium science per batch production cycle
- 200% productivity on Promethium recipes (3x output)
- Ships carry ~330 storage units worth of Promethium chunks

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not skip Promethium science (defeats the purpose of a true megabase)
- Biter egg management is the most dangerous aspect; build fail-safes
- Ships must be fully fueled and defended before launching
- UPS impact is severe; use particle removal mod
