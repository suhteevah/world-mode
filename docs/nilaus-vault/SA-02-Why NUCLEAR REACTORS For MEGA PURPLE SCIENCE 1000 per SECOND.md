## Summary
### Key Lessons
- Purple science is the worst among normal sciences; tackle it first
- Stone is the critical bottleneck for purple science on Vulcanus
- Use nuclear reactor lava-to-stone technique: copper smelting from lava yields 15 stone per iteration
- Recipe flip technique exists for deleting fluids but is "cheating"; brute force copper casting into lava is preferred
- Modular city-block design: one column of city blocks, copy-paste for scaling

### Space Age Mechanics
- Vulcanus lava mechanics: underground belts don't work on lava tiles; minimize dead space
- Calcite + lava = stone production chain for Vulcanus-based builds
- Nuclear reactors provide the lava infrastructure needed for stone production
- 128 rocket launchers simultaneously launching purple science to orbit

### Design Principles
- 92 assemblers per module as baseline; 3 modules = enough production
- Self-contained city block: scrap-in, science-out with internal smelting
- Stacker inserters between boxes handle 120/sec throughput
- Build on lava: fill specific tiles to prevent underground belt issues

### Ratios & Numbers
- 960 purple science/sec achieved (target 1000/sec)
- 333 red circuits/sec needed for purple science
- 1,200 stone/sec needed; 6 foundries produce enough from lava
- 20,000 molten copper/sec byproduct; 18 casters needed to consume it
- Inputs: less than 1 belt each of calcite, coal, and sulfuric acid

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't try to bring stone to orbit for purple/military science (no way to make stone in space)
- Don't forget lava tile restrictions when building on Vulcanus
- Don't use recipe-flip fluid deletion; it's fragile and annoying
- Ensure consistent lava supply before scaling up stone production
