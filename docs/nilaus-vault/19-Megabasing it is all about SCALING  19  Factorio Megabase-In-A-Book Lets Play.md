## Summary
### Key Lessons
- Megabasing is fundamentally about scaling: doubling blue circuits, adding 50% red circuits, doubling plastic, adding 33% green circuits in a single episode
- Scaling up creates a circular dependency: more blue circuits need more modules, which need more blue circuits
- The design phase and the scaling phase are both essential parts of megabase building - neither should be skipped
- Green circuits are the ultimate bottleneck indicator - when they blink on the dashboard, everything downstream suffers
- Spidertrons need rockets to be effective in combat at this stage - without rockets they are just expensive punching bags

### Design Principles
- Alternate between design episodes and scaling episodes for balanced megabase progression
- When scaling, touch every production chain that is limiting: circuits, plastic, intermediates
- Use the dashboard to identify which resource to scale next - fix the blinking lights
- Solar panel expansion should be continuous and parallel on both sides of the base
- Pollution management matters: clear biter bases in the direction pollution travels to prevent constant attacks

### Ratios & Numbers
- Blue circuits: doubled production in this episode
- Red circuits: increased by 50%
- Plastic: doubled production
- Green circuits: increased by 33% (added ~369 green circuits per second)
- Total trains at this point: growing concern for network capacity
- Green circuit production: 8 full blue belts consumed by single build

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Issuing too many construction commands at once overwhelms builder robots - they queue up and delay critical builds
- Running out of inserters during a scale-up session leaves new production non-functional
- Scaling blue circuits requires scaling modules which requires more blue circuits - plan for this circular dependency
- Not clearing biter bases in pollution direction causes constant attacks on the weakest perimeter sections
