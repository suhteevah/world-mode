## Summary

Nilaus gets the train-to-train plastic build fully operational. He discovers a critical fluid wagon connection issue: pumps connect to specific fluid wagon "slots," and you can only attach 3 pumps per wagon, each at different positions. He reconstructs the entire build from scratch to ensure balanced oil distribution, pre-saturating all tanks to 200,000 before enabling coal and plastic trains. Each new plastic facility produces 660 plastic/second compared to 336/s from the old belt-based builds. Three facilities total will replace all old plastic production, and old builds can be decommissioned.

### Key Lessons
- Fluid wagons have only 3 pump connection points, each at a different physical position -- you cannot attach two pumps to the same slot
- Starting a fluid system balanced is critical: fill all tanks completely before enabling production to avoid permanent imbalance
- Train-to-train plastic produces nearly 2x the output per facility vs old belt-based builds (660/s vs 336/s)
- Trains by themselves do not consume many calculations; pathfinding over short distances and idle trains are cheap

### Design Principles
- Pre-saturate oil storage (all tanks to 200,000) before enabling coal input to ensure balanced startup
- Use trains as a fluid balancing medium since balancing fluids across many pipes is "notoriously bad"
- Decommission old production gradually: starve old facilities of coal while new ones ramp up
- Number your train station groups and match train schedules to group numbers for organized multi-facility management

### Ratios & Numbers
- New plastic facility: 660 plastic/second
- Old plastic facility: 336 plastic/second (each)
- 3 new facilities needed to replace all old plastic production
- Oil pre-saturation: 200,000 per tank across all 5 stations
- 12 trains needed for one facility: 12 x 8 = 96 fluid wagons
- 18 coal trains assigned to supply the new builds
- 14 coal trains initially (not enough, had to add 4 more)
- Target: 7,500 SPM next milestone

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not attempt to connect two pumps to the same fluid wagon slot -- they silently fail
- Starting fluid systems with unbalanced oil levels causes permanent production imbalance
- Do not forget to fuel trains before enabling the production loop
- Underestimating coal train count leads to starvation -- always over-provision slightly
