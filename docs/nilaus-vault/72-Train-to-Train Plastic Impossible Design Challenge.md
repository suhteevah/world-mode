## Summary

Nilaus tackles the challenge of designing a train-to-train plastic production system within the constraints of his city-block megabase. The episode reveals why this is considered nearly impossible: plastic requires oil refining (multiple fluid stages), coal input, and water, all needing to fit between two train tracks. He explores multiple layout iterations, struggling with spacing, inserter reach, beacon coverage, and fluid throughput constraints.

### Key Lessons
- Train-to-train designs for multi-input recipes (like plastic) are exponentially harder than single-input ones (like smelting)
- When designing train-to-train, the lower-quantity input (coal) should get the "worse" unloading position since throughput demands are lower
- Consumption statistics in Factorio show actual consumption, not desired consumption -- a starved machine shows zero consumption, which is deceptive
- Processing units (blue circuits) were a bottleneck because trains sent only every ~5-6 minutes due to low volume

### Design Principles
- Train-to-train builds must fit within a 2-track-width cycle for the city-block system
- The coal input train and plastic output train need to be as close as possible to minimize dead space
- Direct inserting from trains is problematic when trains move in and out frequently
- When a design "almost works," iterating small changes can unlock solutions -- but sometimes you hit a brick wall and need community help

### Ratios & Numbers
- Blue circuits: producing 5.8/s, consuming ~4/s, but train dispatch only every ~5-6 minutes
- Sulfuric acid: pumping ~1000/s per pump, threshold at 100k to open, battery doubling caused consumption spike
- Battery storage threshold: 192,000 before production stops
- Plastic prototype throughput: only 118/s initially, needed 81/s consumption rate

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not assume consumption graphs reflect actual demand -- they only show what is actually being consumed
- Avoid building direct-insert from trains when trains cycle frequently (items get lost during movement)
- Do not start building before confirming the design tiles correctly
- Sulfuric acid demand can spike unexpectedly when you double battery production
