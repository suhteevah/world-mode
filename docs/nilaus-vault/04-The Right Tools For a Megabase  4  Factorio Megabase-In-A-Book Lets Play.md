## Summary
### Key Lessons
- Before continuing with production builds, invest in infrastructure tools: automatic refueling, faster construction, and landfill capability
- A dedicated fuel/logistics train solves two problems at once: refueling outpost trains AND restocking logistics robots at each city block
- The fuel train uses "inactivity" as its wait condition (not "full" or "empty") because it may not always need to fully load/unload
- Spidertrons ("build-a-trons") dramatically speed up construction of remote outposts compared to personal roboports

### Design Principles
- Fuel train carries nuclear fuel AND logistics robots in filtered wagons (1-2 wagon train, not full 1-8-1)
- Each outpost's fuel station uses a circuit condition: when logistics network fuel count drops below threshold (e.g., <5), enable the station to summon the fuel train
- Inserters at fuel stations are filtered to only insert specific items (nuclear fuel to requester chest, logistics bots to roboport)
- Use the roboport's logistics network reading to monitor fuel/bot levels and control station enable/disable
- Spidertrons loaded with construction materials follow waypoints to build queued blueprints remotely

### Ratios & Numbers
- Fuel train: 1 locomotive + 2 wagons (one for nuclear fuel stacks of 8 per slot, one for logistics bots at ~100 per slot)
- Fuel station threshold: enable when nuclear fuel in logistics network < 5-10 units
- Logistics bots per outpost roboport: ~10-50 depending on outpost complexity
- Spidertron construction squad: 2-4 spidertrons with personal roboports and construction materials

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not use "until full" wait condition on fuel trains - if there aren't enough items to fill up, the train will be stuck forever
- Do not use unfiltered inserters at fuel stations - they will dump anything into chests
- Do not let roboport networks from adjacent city blocks merge accidentally - keep them isolated
- Do not neglect landfill production; expansion over water requires massive amounts and it is easy to run out
