## Summary
### Key Lessons
- Science production at megabase scale means building science you cannot yet consume - production far outpaces initial lab capacity
- Oil supply setup: use existing liquid provider blueprints, connect pump jacks to fluid wagons with max 200,000 buffer
- Red science at 5,400/min is the first mega-scale science target - requires 4 iron belts in, produces 90/sec theoretical max
- Blue circuits remain the persistent bottleneck throughout scaling - every new build draws more blue circuits
- Dashboard alerts help identify which resources are truly limiting vs. which are intentionally disabled

### Design Principles
- Build science production blocks even before labs can consume them - stockpile for when all 7 sciences are online
- Use the dashboard to selectively disable alerts for resources not yet in production (stone bricks, unbuilt sciences)
- Oil supply: set train limit to 2 max at fluid loading stations to prevent mainline blocking
- Science train: goes "until full" at production, then waits at destination (no return trip until labs exist)
- Red science needs: iron + copper inbound, 4 lanes each, output on 2 belts

### Ratios & Numbers
- Red science target: 5,400 per minute (90 per second)
- Oil pump output: ~1,400-1,600 per pump, buffer target 200,000 per station
- Red science train capacity: 8,000 per wagon x 4 wagons = 32,000 per train
- Blue circuit consumption will spike when science trains start cycling - plan for 2x current blue circuit production
- Iron/copper draw: 4 additional lanes per science build, may cause temporary deficit in base supply

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Building oil station without verifying train path connectivity first (caused "no path" errors)
- Setting science train station to wrong capacity thresholds - must match actual wagon x stack calculations
- Forgetting to add fueling to new train stations leaves trains stranded
- Do not panic about dashboard alerts for resources you intentionally have not built yet - disable those alerts
