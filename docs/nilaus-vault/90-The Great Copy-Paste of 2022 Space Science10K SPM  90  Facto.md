## Summary

### Key Lessons
- Plastic warnings persisted despite calculated surplus because coal train delivery was the actual bottleneck, not plastic production capacity
- Debugging supply chain issues requires tracing backward from the warning: plastic shortage -> coal train shortage -> insufficient coal trains for southern oil facilities
- "The Great Copy-Paste" refers to duplicating the entire space science infrastructure for 10K SPM; at this scale, copy-paste of proven designs is the fastest scaling method
- Coal trains being missing from distant plastic stations is a train allocation problem, not a production problem
- When supply shows "+5 available" but stations are starving, check the delivery chain (coal -> oil -> plastic) for the real bottleneck

### Design Principles
- Always trace warnings to root cause: a plastic warning may actually be a coal train shortage
- Check the furthest stations first when debugging intermittent supply issues; distance amplifies train allocation problems
- Copy-paste scaling of proven builds is the right approach for space science at 10K; do not redesign what already works
- Train limit signals (showing "0 of 1") at stations indicate the station wants a train but none are available in the network
- Fix coal/fuel delivery infrastructure before scaling plastic-dependent production

### Ratios & Numbers
- Plastic supply: +13 idle trains in network (seemingly healthy)
- Plastic demand: only 3 unfulfilled demands (seemingly minor)
- Root cause: coal trains missing from southern oil facilities; coal train limit showing "0 of 1" at multiple stations
- Plastic station example: 20,000 out of 32,000 items, should be filling but stalled due to no coal input
- Space science: being scaled to 10K SPM via copy-paste of existing rocket silo + satellite infrastructure

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not assume a plastic warning means you need more plastic production; check the coal/oil supply chain first
- Do not ignore "0 of 1" train limit signals; they indicate a train network allocation failure
- Do not assume distant stations get the same service as close ones; travel time means distant stations need proportionally more trains
- Verify coal delivery to ALL oil processing facilities, especially newly built southern ones
