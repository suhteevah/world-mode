## Summary
### Key Lessons
- Goal is 1,000,000 science per minute for research productivity (not skipping hard sciences like Gleba/Promethium)
- Legendary biolabs at +700% productivity: 1 science pack = 16 science (target: +900% = 1 pack = 20 science)
- At +900% productivity, need 50,000 science packs/min = ~833/sec = 4 full belts per science type
- Use robot-fed biolabs with legendary science packs to reduce robot load by 6x
- Science monitor blueprint with Alt+Right-Click map pins for cross-planet visibility

### Space Age Mechanics
- Legendary biolabs consume at half speed, doubling effective value
- Legendary science packs are worth 6x normal, reducing belt/robot throughput needs
- 12 different science types all need megabase-level production
- Agricultural science requires spoilage handling in biolab design

### Design Principles
- Modular tileable biolab design: 2-block repeatable units feeding 12 science lines
- Feed from one side, flow through; agricultural science on top row for spoilage output
- Groups of 4 biolab modules with roboport coverage between groups
- 16x16 layout = 256 biolab rows = ~887K SPM at current tech; expandable to 2M SPM

### Ratios & Numbers
- 128 legendary biolabs target
- Half belt = 120 items/sec; each biolab row consumes 3.7/sec
- 32 biolabs needed to consume 120/sec (one half belt)
- 8 rows per side, mirrored = potential 8 full belts throughput

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Don't use splitters for science input to biolabs (items can get stuck)
- Don't skip the spoilage filtering for agricultural science line
- Don't underestimate robot travel distance; keep roboport coverage tight
- Filter all requester chests to prevent wrong-quality items mixing
