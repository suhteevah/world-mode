## Summary

### Key Lessons
- Scaling science production is done by doubling existing builds (5,400 to 10,800 red/green science per minute by copy-pasting the same blueprint)
- Dedicated train routes vs shared routes is a real trade-off; dedicated circuits for blue science consume specific red/green circuit allocations
- When scaling, it is better to overbuild slightly than to be short on capacity; idle overcapacity costs little in UPS
- Draining old stations of inventory before rebuilding them is tedious but necessary; setting train limits to zero stops new deliveries

### Design Principles
- One train per station plus extras for travel time is the baseline train allocation rule
- Splitters and half-empty belts are UPS-expensive; full belts with minimal splitting are preferable
- When converting dedicated stations to shared (or vice versa), plan the transition carefully to avoid stranding trains or inventory
- Use spidertrons and construction robots together for large-scale builds; send spidertrons ahead to place ghosts, then follow with robots

### Ratios & Numbers
- Red/green science: 5,400/min per build, doubled to 10,800/min with two builds
- Blue science target: 8,100/min (three builds at 2,700 each)
- Blue circuits: 5,400/min existing production, scaling to 8,100/min
- Red circuits: 123/min per module, 246/min from two dedicated builds; blue science demands ~145/min
- Green circuits: one green circuit build (~169/min) supports two red circuit builds (~176/min consumed)
- Steel stations: 68 stops with 48 trains assigned (scaled from 16 trains)
- UPS went from 35 to 45 after adding capacity and fixing bottlenecks

### Mistakes to Avoid
- Do not stand near deconstruction zones when using area deconstruction; you will pick up items and fill your inventory
- Do not forget to check that ghost buildings actually get placed; spidertron robots may claim the job but fail to deliver
- Avoid creating dedicated train routes unless absolutely necessary; they fragment the network and complicate scaling
- Do not ignore dashboard warnings even if they seem minor; plastic shortages cascade into blue circuit shortages
